Seller

In a blockchain-based vaccine supply chain, the role of a seller is similar to that in a traditional supply chain, but with added benefits and responsibilities due to the use of blockchain technology. A seller in a blockchain-based vaccine supply chain is responsible for distributing vaccines to healthcare providers, clinics, pharmacies, and other institutions that administer vaccines. However, with the use of blockchain technology, the seller can ensure that the vaccine supply chain is transparent, secure, and tamper-proof. The seller is responsible for recording all vaccine-related transactions on the blockchain, including the movement of vaccines from the manufacturer to the distributor, and from the distributor to the end-user. The seller also plays a crucial role in ensuring the integrity of the vaccine data on the blockchain by verifying that all transactions are valid and accurate. In addition, the seller may also be responsible for managing smart contracts that are used to automate certain aspects of the vaccine supply chain, such as tracking inventory levels and managing orders.
