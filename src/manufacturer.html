In a vaccine supply chain, a consumer is typically the end-user who receives the vaccine. The role of the consumer is to receive the vaccine and follow the instructions provided by the healthcare provider administering the vaccine, such as scheduling follow-up appointments and reporting any adverse reactions. However, in a blockchain-based vaccine supply chain, the role of the consumer can be expanded to include more active participation in the supply chain. With the use of blockchain technology, consumers can have greater access to information about the vaccines they receive, including the origin and chain of custody of the vaccine. Consumers can also help ensure the integrity and accuracy of vaccine-related data on the blockchain by reporting any discrepancies or errors they observe in the vaccine-related information they receive. This can help improve the transparency and reliability of the vaccine supply chain data, which can in turn improve public trust and confidence in the vaccine supply chain.
